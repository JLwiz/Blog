Color Theory and Web Design

09/20/2021

Color, though a basic aspect of a website, can be one of the most important aspects to any website. By learning more about color theory, it can be used to create more dynamic and cohesive color palettes to be used in designing a website. Colors have a wide range of values for an expansive vocabulary of representations and combinations to explore and play with. Though their is a plethora of possible color combinations, there is four main schools of thought in the creating harmony with colors. With an analogous color layout colors next to eachother in the color wheel are used to create a relatively monotoned, but harmonious, scheen with a predominant color theme. Complementary color schemes offer stark contrast of opposing colors on the color wheel in which they vibrantly oppose one another. Complementary colors, though having high contrast to one another,typically do not create a cohesive aesthetic due to the opposing values and contrasts of the composed palette. Monochromatic schemes provide familiar palettes from page to page; however, that familiarity also provides the draw back of possibly making the web page to feel bland and monotonous - completely opposite to what is provided from complementary colors. For a more balanced color scheme, a triadic scheme uses three colors on the wheel to create a diverse and harmonious composition of colors.
